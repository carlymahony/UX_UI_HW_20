Website Redesign: The Christmas Box International

A webpage redesign for a Salt Lake City non-profit organization that helps children who are victims of abuse.

Research

We performed user interviews in the research phase of our design process to learn more about what our users look for when deciding upon a nonprofit to support. A few key insights that we pulled out of these interviews were:
    1. Our users are interested in participating and anf learning more about charity event
    2. Our users typically donate around the holiday and typically make an annual contribution
    3. Our users are most interested in nonprofits that help children and teens.

Problem Statement

The problem that Sariah, our user persona, faces is that she wants to volunteer with a nonprofit that helps children in need but she lacks the resources to find an organization whose mission aligns with the issues that she feels most passionately. “I want to volunteer with a nonprofit to help children but I lack the resources and direction to find an organization that aligns with my goals.”

Iterations

Iteration #1

Our users liked the idea of having a choice to choose from one time, monthly, and annual donations. Our users pointed out that the smaller donations should be the first options since they were more likely to generate monthly giving.

Iteration #2

The High-fidelity prototype incorporated the feedback on payment frequency and the order of payment amount. Our users also expressed that they would appreciate if the donation page was split into steps so we incorporated that user feedback.

Iteration for Mobile

The same feedback that we received on the webpage payment form was incorporated in the mobile app expereince. Users are able to select from monthly, annual or one-time payments. Additionally payment amount are sorted from smallest to largest.
